INDIAN CUISINES

CHANA MASALA

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 (1 inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, chopped, or more to taste
  • 1 green chile pepper, seeded and chopped (Optional)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 fresh bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 pinch salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • 1 (15 ounce) can chickpeas
  • 1 teaspoon fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish, or more to taste

DIRECTIONS

  1. Grind onion, tomato, ginger, garlic, and chile pepper together in a food processor into a paste.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Fry bay leaves until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  3. Pour the paste into the skillet and cook until the oil begins to separate from the mixture and is golden brown in color, 2 to 3 minutes.
  4. Season the mixture with chili powder, coriander, garam masala, turmeric, and salt; cook and stir until very hot, 2 to 3 minutes.
  5. Stir enough water into the mixture to get a thick gravy; bring to a boil and stir chickpeas into the gravy.
  6. Reduce heat to medium and cook until the chickpeas are heated through, 5 to 7 minutes.
  7. Garnish with cilantro.

RAJMA

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups dry red kidney beans
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1 (2 inch) piece fresh ginger root, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2 dried red chile peppers, broken into pieces
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 6 whole cloves
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground red pepper
  • ¼ cup cilantro leaves, chopped

DIRECTIONS

  1. Place the kidney beans into a large container and cover with several inches of cool water; let stand 8 hours or overnight. Drain and rinse.
  2. Grind the onion, ginger, and garlic into a paste using a mortar and pestle.
  3. Heat the oil and ghee together in a pressure cooker over medium heat. Fry the red chile peppers, cumin seeds, and whole cloves in the hot oil until the cumin seeds begin to splutter.
  4. Stir the onion paste into the mixture and cook, stirring frequently, until golden brown. Season with the ground turmeric, ground cumin, and ground coriander; continue cooking for a few more seconds before adding the tomatoes. Cook until the tomatoes are completely tender.
  5. Add the drained kidney beans to the pressure cooker with enough water to cover; pour the 2 cups water additionally to the cooker. Add the sugar and salt.
  6. Close the pressure cooker and bring to 15 pounds of pressure; cook about 40 minutes. Lower the heat to low and cook another 10 to 15 minutes.
  7. Release the pressure and open the cooker. Stir the garam masala and ground red pepper into the bean mixture; garnish with chopped cilantro to serve.

KOFTA

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 3 green cardamom pods
  • 2 whole cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 bay leaf
  • ½ onion, grated
  • 1 (2 inch) piece ginger root, peeled and grated
  • 1 clove garlic, crushed
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • ½ teaspoon ground turmeric
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon garam masala
  • ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper (Optional)
  • ½ teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • ¼ cup whole milk yogurt
  • 24 frozen meatless vegetable meatballs (such as IKEA®'s frozen vegetable balls)
  • ¼ cup hot water, or as needed

DIRECTIONS

  1. Heat olive oil and butter in a skillet over medium heat. Fry cumin seeds, cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon stick, and bay leaf in the hot oil and butter until fragrant.
  2. Stir grated onion into the spice mixture; cook and stir until onion begins to brown, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add ginger and garlic paste to the onion mixture; cook and stir until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  4. Stir diced tomatoes into the onion mixture; cook and stir until tomatoes break down and become sauce-like, about 10 minutes.
  5. Season the sauce with coriander, turmeric, ground cumin, garam masala, cayenne pepper, and salt; stir well.
  6. Stir yogurt into the sauce; add vegetable meatballs and hot water.
  7. Reduce heat to low and simmer until the flavors meld together, about 30 minutes.

BUTTER CHICKEN

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • ¾ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 3 boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-size pieces
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, finely chopped
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 (8 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup chopped fresh cilantro

DIRECTIONS

  1. Combine yogurt, lemon juice, 2 teaspoons cumin, cinnamon, cayenne, black pepper, ginger, and 2 teaspoons salt in a large bowl. Stir in chicken, cover, and refrigerate for 1 hour.
  2. Melt butter in a large heavy skillet over medium heat. Cook and stir garlic and jalapeno for 1 minute. Season with 2 teaspoons cumin, paprika, and 3 teaspoons salt. Stir in tomato sauce and cream. Simmer on low heat until sauce thickens, about 20 minutes.
  3. Preheat a grill for high heat.
  4. Lightly oil the grill grate. Thread chicken onto skewers, and discard marinade. Grill until juices run clear, about 5 minutes on each side.
  5. Add grilled chicken to the sauce, and continue to simmer for 10 minutes. Transfer to a serving platter, and garnish with fresh cilantro.

PANEER TIKKA MASALA

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon minced fresh ginger
  • 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 3 boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-size pieces
  • 4 long skewers
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, finely chopped
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 (8 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro

DIRECTIONS

  1. In a large bowl, combine yogurt, lemon juice, 2 teaspoons cumin, cinnamon, cayenne, black pepper, ginger, and 1 teaspoon salt. Stir in chicken, cover, and refrigerate for 1 hour.
  2. Preheat a grill for high heat.
  3. Lightly oil the grill grate. Thread chicken onto skewers, and discard marinade. Grill until juices run clear, about 5 minutes on each side.
  4. Melt butter in a large heavy skillet over medium heat. Saute garlic and jalapeno for 1 minute. Season with 2 teaspoons cumin, paprika, and 3 teaspoons salt. Stir in tomato sauce and cream. Simmer on low heat until sauce thickens, about 20 minutes. Add grilled chicken, and simmer for 10 minutes. Transfer to a serving platter, and garnish with fresh cilantro.

PALAK PANEER

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1 (1 inch) piece ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 (14.5 ounce) can diced tomatoes, drained
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 pound paneer, cut into cubes
  • 1 bunch fresh spinach, stems removed
  • 1 cup heavy cream

DIRECTIONS

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Saute onion until lightly browned, 5 to 10 minutes. Stir in garlic and ginger; cook for 1 minute. Add cumin, coriander, turmeric, paprika, cinnamon, black pepper, nutmeg, and cardamom; cook and stir for 1 minute.
  2. Stir drained tomatoes into the skillet; cook until heated through, about 5 minutes. Transfer mixture to a blender or food processor. Puree until smooth.
  3. Return tomato mixture to the skillet. Stir water into the mixture, adding more if needed to reach your desired consistency. Bring to a simmer. Add paneer and spinach; simmer until heated through, about 5 minutes.
  4. Stir cream into the skillet; simmer until heated through, 3 to 5 minutes.
